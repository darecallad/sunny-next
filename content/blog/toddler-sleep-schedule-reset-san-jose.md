The holidays are over. The decorations are coming down. But there's one thing that's still lingering: **The Holiday Sleep Hangover.**

Maybe you let them stay up late for New Year's Eve. Maybe naps were skipped during family parties. Now, it's January in **San Jose**, school is starting, and your toddler is wide awake at 10 PM and cranky at 7 AM.

Don't panic. You don't need to fix it in one night. At Sunny Child Care, we recommend a gentle **3-Day Reset** to shift their internal clock back to "school mode."

### Day 1: The Morning Wake-Up Call (The Hardest Part)
You can't force a child to fall asleep, but you *can* control when they wake up.
*   **The Strategy:** Wake them up 15-30 minutes earlier than their "holiday wake-up time," even if they are tired.
*   **Why:** This builds "sleep pressure" for the evening.
*   **Tip:** Open the curtains immediately. Sunlight is the strongest signal to the brain that the day has started.

### Day 2: Cap the Nap
If they are tired from waking up early, they might want a mega-nap. Don't let them sleep too long!
*   **The Strategy:** Cap the afternoon nap at 1.5 or 2 hours (depending on age). Do not let them sleep past 3:00 PM.
*   **Why:** A late nap steals sleep from bedtime.
*   **Activity:** Go for a walk in a local park like **Alum Rock Park** or just around the neighborhood in the late afternoon. The fresh air helps reset the circadian rhythm.

### Day 3: The Bedtime Routine Re-Boot
By Day 3, their body should be tired earlier. Now, re-introduce your strict bedtime ritual.
*   **The Strategy:** Start the routine 30 minutes earlier than usual.
*   **The Routine:** Bath -> Pajamas -> Story -> Bed.
*   **No Screens:** Turn off iPads and TVs at least an hour before bed. Blue light suppresses melatonin.

### Be Patient with "Jet Lag"
Treat this post-holiday phase like jet lag. They might be grumpy. They might cry. Validate their feelings: *"I know you're tired. It's hard to wake up."* But hold the boundary.

Consistency is love. By getting them back on track, you are giving them the rest they need to grow, learn, and play happily at daycare.

假期結束了，裝飾品也收起來了。但有一樣東西還賴著不走：**「假期睡眠宿醉」**。

也許跨年夜你們讓孩子熬夜了；也許家庭聚會時午睡被跳過了。現在是一月，**San Jose** 的學校開學了，但你的幼兒在晚上 10 點還精神百倍，早上 7 點卻起床氣爆發。

別慌張。你不需要在一個晚上解決這個問題。在 Sunny Child Care，我們建議使用溫和的**「3 天重置法」**，將他們的生理時鐘調回「上學模式」。

### 第一天：早晨的喚醒號角（最難的一步）
你不能強迫孩子睡著，但你*可以*控制他們什麼時候醒來。
*   **策略：** 比他們的「假期起床時間」早叫醒他們 15-30 分鐘，即使他們很累。
*   **原因：** 這會為晚上累積「睡眠壓力」。
*   **小撇步：** 馬上拉開窗簾。陽光是告訴大腦「一天開始了」最強烈的訊號。

### 第二天：限制午睡時間
如果他們因為早起而感到疲累，他們可能會想睡個超長的午覺。別讓他們睡太久！
*   **策略：** 將午睡限制在 1.5 或 2 小時（視年齡而定）。絕對不要讓他們睡超過下午 3:00。
*   **原因：** 太晚的午睡會偷走晚上的睡眠時間。
*   **活動：** 下午晚些時候去附近的公園（如 **Alum Rock Park**）或社區走走。新鮮空氣有助於重置晝夜節律。

### 第三天：睡前儀式重啟
到了第三天，他們的身體應該會比較早感到疲累。現在，重新嚴格執行你的睡前儀式。
*   **策略：** 比平常提早 30 分鐘開始儀式。
*   **儀式：** 洗澡 -> 穿睡衣 -> 說故事 -> 上床。
*   **無螢幕：** 睡前至少一小時關掉 iPad 和電視。藍光會抑制褪黑激素的分泌。

### 對待「時差」要有耐心
把這個收假階段當作「調時差」。他們可能會脾氣暴躁，可能會哭鬧。同理他們的感受：*「我知道你很累，起床很辛苦。」* 但要堅守界線。

規律就是愛。透過幫助他們回到正軌，你給了他們成長、學習和在托兒所快樂玩耍所需的休息。
