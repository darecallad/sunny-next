The first week of January is always the hardest.
You pull up to the school drop-off zone in **San Jose**. You unbuckle the car seat. And then it starts. The clinging. The crying. The pleading: *"No school! I want to stay home with you!"*

It breaks your heart. You might feel guilty, thinking, *Did I spoil them too much over the break?*

The answer is no. You just gave them two weeks of love, attention, and fun. Of course they want that to continue! This "regression" is completely normal. Their world shifted for the holidays, and now it's shifting back. That transition is scary.

Here are three ways to help your child (and yourself) navigate the "Back-to-School Blues."

### 1. Validate, Don't Distract
When a child is crying, our instinct is to say, "Look! There's a toy!" or "Don't cry, it's okay."
But this tells them their feelings are wrong.
**Try this instead:** Validate the feeling.
*"I know. You loved being home with Mommy and Daddy. It's hard to say goodbye. I miss you too."*
When they feel heard, they don't have to scream louder to get your attention.

### 2. The "Pocket Kiss" (The Transitional Object)
Give them a physical connection to hold onto.
It could be a small family photo laminated for their pocket. Or, try the "Pocket Kiss" (inspired by the book *The Kissing Hand*).
**The Ritual:** Kiss their palm, close their fingers over it, and say: *"I'm putting a kiss right here. Whenever you miss me, put your hand to your cheek, and you'll feel my love."*
It sounds cheesy, but for a toddler, this magic is real.

### 3. The "Quick & Confident" Goodbye
This is the hardest part for parents.
Once you say goodbye, **leave**.
If you linger, look sad, or come back for "one more hug," you signal to your child: *Mommy is worried. Maybe this place isn't safe.*
**The Strategy:** Be confident. Smile (even if you fake it). Say: *"I love you. I will be back after snack time. Have fun!"* And walk away.
Trust the teachers. Usually, the tears stop 2 minutes after you leave.

### It Gets Easier
This week is just a bump in the road. Routine is comforting to children, and they will settle back into it faster than you think.
Be patient with the tears this week—theirs and yours. You are doing a great job.

一月的第一週總是最難熬的。
你把車開到 **San Jose** 的學校接送區。你解開安全座椅的扣環。然後，戲碼開始了。緊抓不放。大哭。哀求：*「不要上學！我要跟你待在家裡！」*

這讓人心碎。你可能會感到內疚，想著：*我是不是在假期裡把他們寵壞了？*

答案是：沒有。你只是給了他們兩週的愛、關注和快樂。他們當然希望這一切能繼續！這種「倒退」是完全正常的。他們的世界在假期裡改變了，現在又要變回來。這種轉換是令人害怕的。

這裡有三個方法，幫你的孩子（和你自己）度過這場「收假症候群」。

### 1. 同理情緒，而不是轉移注意力
當孩子哭的時候，我們的本能是說：「看！那裡有玩具！」或是「不要哭，沒事的。」
但这等於告訴他們：你的感受是錯的。
**試試這招：** 同理他們的感受。
*「我知道。你很喜歡跟爸爸媽媽待在家裡。說再見真的很難。我也會想你。」*
當他們覺得被聽見了，就不需要用更大的尖叫聲來引起你的注意。

### 2. 「口袋裡的親親」（過渡性客體）
給他們一個可以抓得住的實體連結。
那可以是一張護貝好的小全家福照片，放在他們的口袋裡。或者，試試「口袋裡的親親」（靈感來自繪本《魔法親親 The Kissing Hand》）。
**儀式：** 親一下他們的手心，把他們的手指合起來包住它，然後說：*「我把一個親親放在這裡。當你想我的時候，把手貼在臉頰上，你就會感覺到我的愛。」*
這聽起來很肉麻，但對幼兒來說，這種魔法是真的。

### 3. 「快速且自信」的道別
這對父母來說是最難的部分。
一旦你說了再見，**就離開**。
如果你在那裡徘徊、看起來很傷心，或是跑回來「再抱最後一次」，你是在對孩子發出訊號：*媽媽很擔心。也許這裡不安全。*
**策略：** 要有自信。微笑（即使你是裝的）。說：*「我愛你。吃完點心後我就會回來接你。玩得開心！」* 然後轉身離開。
相信老師。通常，在你離開兩分鐘後，眼淚就停了。

### 一切會好轉的
這週只是一個小小的過渡期。規律的作息對孩子來說是有安撫作用的，他們重新適應的速度會比你想像的還快。
這週對眼淚多一點耐心吧——無論是孩子的，還是你自己的。你已經做得很好了。
